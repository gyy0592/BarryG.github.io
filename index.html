About Me

Hello! My name is YiYang (Barry) Guo. I am a passionate and dedicated gravitational wave data analyst in training. Currently, I’m pursuing an integrated Bachelor’s and Master’s degree in Theoretical Physics at Lanzhou University, specializing in gravitational wave data analysis. My research focuses on leveraging advanced computational methods and machine learning techniques to detect and analyze gravitational wave signals, particularly those from binary systems and extreme-mass-ratio inspirals (EMRIs).

I have a strong interest in applying machine learning algorithms to astrophysical data, aiming to improve the accuracy and efficiency of detecting non-stationary noise patterns in gravitational wave detectors. I’ve had the privilege of working with renowned professors in the field, including collaborations with Professor Soumya D. Mohanty from the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley and Professor WenBiao Han at Shanghai Astronomical Observatory.
