About Me

Hello! My name is YiYang (Barry) Guo. I am a passionate and dedicated gravitational wave data analyst in training. Currently, I'm pursuing an integrated Bachelor's and Master's degree at Lanzhou University, specializing in gravitational wave data analysis. My research involves the application of machine learning to gravitational wave data analysis, which is my top interest, focusing on the search and extraction of signals from binary white dwarf systems. Additionally, I examine the impact of parameter estimation in systems involving extreme-mass-ratio inspirals (EMRIs) and white dwarfs.

I am currently working on a project using machine learning to handle noise from ground-based gravitational wave detectors. I've had the privilege of collaborating with Professor Soumya D. Mohanty from the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing tennis, swimming, and competing in League of Legends(Diamond level). I'm also a rock music lover, with favorite bands including Queen, Fleetwood Mac, and Maroon 5.
